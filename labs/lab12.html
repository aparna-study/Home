California Drought

SAN FRANCISCO (KRON) — The Bay Area Council has released what it found to be the top 5 issues concerning Bay Area residents, and the results may be surprising. The poll asked Bay Area residents to identify issues government leaders should focus on. Placing No. 1, 48 percent believed the drought was the top concern, according to the poll.
Bay Area municipalities along with the rest of state are facing strict water restrictions that Governor Jerry Brown approved several weeks ago. California is in the midst of a crippling 4-year drought.


The drought destroying California’s farmlands and parching its wells has another consequence: dirtier air. Industrial cities in the Golden State are seeing higher levels of air pollution as a result of warmer, drier weather, a trend that is undermining statewide efforts to clean up emissions from power plants and tailpipes. “Climate is playing a big role in the quality of air and impact on public health,” said Janice Nolen of the American Lung Association, in Washington. “As places have warmer temperatures, it’s harder to keep [pollution] under control.”
